The Brains: Edge AI on Raspberry Pi 4

The choice of a Raspberry Pi creates a "constraint cascade." The limited processing power forces a trade-off between the object detection model's accuracy and its speed, which directly impacts the rover's operational velocity.

This chart compares the inference time of different object detection models on a Raspberry Pi without a hardware accelerator. Lower is better. YOLOv8n offers a modern balance, but its speed (~1-2 FPS) limits the rover's physical speed, highlighting the need for optimization or a hardware accelerator like a Google Coral TPU.

Manipulation: Grasping Irregular Objects

Litter comes in all shapes and sizes. A simple rigid pincer fails on many common items. A compliant, 3D-printed gripper design offers the best combination of versatility, robustness, and low cost.

A radar chart comparing low-cost gripper designs on key metrics for litter collection. The 3D-Printed Soft Gripper (like a Fin Ray) shows the most balanced profile, excelling in adaptability and cost-effectiveness, making it the ideal choice.

Operational Logic: From Patrol to Exploration

Instead of following a rigid, predefined path, the rover can operate more intelligently by freely exploring its environment. This "free-roaming" approach is better for finding randomly scattered litter but requires a more robust reactive navigation system.

Cost-Benefit Analysis: The Economic Case

The primary driver for automation is economic. A low-cost robotic prototype represents a capital expenditure that is vastly cheaper than the recurring operational cost of manual labor, promising a rapid return on investment.

A single rover prototype can be built for less than one month's salary of a manual sanitation worker in India. A fleet of such rovers, operating 24/7, offers a compelling long-term economic advantage for municipalities and private campuses.

Key Challenges & Future Directions

Current Limitations

  • Environmental Adversity: Variable lighting, shadows, and rain severely impact vision-based perception and navigation.
  • Feature-Poor Terrain: Open fields or sandy areas lack distinct features, causing Visual SLAM to fail and localization to drift.
  • Perception-Speed Bottleneck: The Pi's CPU limits detection speed, forcing the rover to move slowly to be effective.
  • Manipulation Accuracy: A simple 2-DOF arm has limited reach and dexterity, reducing grasp success rate.

Future Research

  • Enhanced Sensing: Fusing vision with LiDAR or using stereo cameras would provide robust depth data, overcoming lighting issues.
  • Swarm Intelligence: Deploying a team of simpler, coordinated rovers can clean large areas more efficiently than a single complex robot.
  • Lifelong Learning: Developing systems that learn from experience in their environment to improve detection and grasping over time.
  • Human-Robot Interaction: Creating intuitive interfaces for managing fleets of rovers and ensuring safe operation in public spaces.
